About SproutScribe

We’re a student research team from Raffles Institution (RI) exploring sustainable writing instruments as a secondary school science project. Our mission: reduce the plastic footprint of disposable pens by designing a pen that writes smoothly, biodegrades safely, and can be planted to grow into a plant at end-of-life.

SproutScribe uses a plant-based body reinforced with natural fibers, non-toxic vegetable inks, and a seed capsule in the cap. When the ink is used up, you plant the cap and return the pen body to compost — turning single-use habits into hands-on sustainability.

Innovative Features

100% Biodegradable Body

Proprietary plant-based blend (PLA + bamboo fiber) for durability during use and breakdown in compost.

Seed Capsule in Cap

Non-GMO seeds enclosed for planting after use. Cap balances moisture protection and easy opening.

Smooth, Safe Ink

Vegetable-based pigments tuned for flow and stability. Writing performance comparable to regular ballpoints.

The Science

Materials Research

We evaluated biodegradable polymers and natural fibers to achieve a body that resists classroom wear but breaks down in compost. Best candidate: PLA reinforced with bamboo fibers.

0–2 months15% degraded
2–4 months60% degraded
4–6 months92% degraded

Testing Methodology

Decomposition tested under three conditions: industrial compost, home compost, and landfill-like environments. Optimal breakdown occurs in compost.

  • No toxic residues detected post-decomposition
  • Seed germination: 78% from cap-embedded seeds
  • Writing performance comparable to conventional pens

FAQ

Any questions?

Yes — we compiled every question received and answered them below.

NA / nil?

When responses said “NA” or “nil”, we treated them as no question.

How to open/close pen?

Prototype A uses a twist; Prototype B uses a click top. Both avoid metal springs to keep parts compostable.

Where’s the cap?

The cap doubles as a seed capsule. It’s moisture-protected but easy to open for planting.

How much will it cost?

Target: similar to mid-range school pens. Bulk school packs and refills keep costs competitive.

How long will the pen last?

Comparable to a standard ballpoint in normal use. Biodegradation starts in composting conditions, not in your pencil case.

Where’s the grip?

A textured bamboo-fiber sleeve provides grip without synthetic rubber. We’re testing shapes for comfort.

What if I don’t want the seed?

Opt for the seed-free cap or gift the seed to a friend/community garden.

Would this cost more than a normal pen?

Materials can be slightly higher, but in bulk and with refills the total cost approaches regular pens.

What’s the point of the seed in the pen?

It turns disposal into learning: plant the cap, track germination, and reflect on circular design.

Why buy this if a normal pen is cheaper?

Lower plastic waste, safer inks, educational value, and a positive end-of-life story.
